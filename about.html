About

Hi, I'm Bríain (that’s me over to the right)!

From a young age, I’ve been fascinated by what makes things tick. This curiosity led me to Dublin City University, where I am now finishing my Master’s in Mechatronic Engineering. A turning point came during my internship with Pfizer, where I invested in my first 3D printer (Elegoo Neptune 4 Pro) and a soldering iron. This allowed me to start moving from theory to reality, transforming ideas into prototypes like my LED matrix lamp and a custom flight controller built around the Arduino Pro Mini.

Beyond my personal projects, my professional foundation is built on a mix of technical depth and operational leadership...

When I’m away from the workstation, you’ll likely find me on a hurling pitch. I’ve been a member of the GAA since I was four years old, and it’s always been a massive part of my life. One of my proudest moments was captaining my team to a championship final, an experience that taught me a lot about leadership and staying calm under pressure. Recently, I’ve also taken the opportunity to travel, exploring cities like Budapest, Munich, and Edinburgh. Last summer, I spent four months working and traveling across America, where I picked up a camera and started exploring photography. You can see a small collection of my favourite shots below.
