MiniCopter

Redesigning conventional quadcopters with basic components.

Type Personal Project
Timeline 2025 - Present
Role Designer

The Mission

Design and develop a 3D-printed FPV micro-drone based on the 3.3V Arduino Pro Mini.

  • Hardware: A minimalist, easy-to-assemble PCB integrating MPU6050 stabilization and NRF24L01 telemetry with a 3D printed frame
  • Software: A custom coded control suite and communication protocol designed for immediate deployment and stability.

Design & Iteration

Circuit Design

I designed a dual sided PCB to integrate the Arduino Pro Mini and peripheral modules into a compact, weight optimised system. Mirroring the MPU-6050 and Arduino enabled direct pin mating, which minimised I2C trace lengths and improved signal integrity. To reduce component count and mass, the NRF24L01 is powered via the MPU’s onboard LDO regulator. The motor drivers are isolated at the board’s periphery to prevent high frequency switching noise from contaminating the IMU’s sensitive data.

Chassis Composition

The chassis features a modular three-part assembly, base frame, PCB mount, and camera mount, optimised for FDM 3D printing and secured via M2 fasteners with threaded brass inserts. An inverted “press-fit” motor configuration enables an integrated propeller guard, providing a protective landing base that isolates the motors from ground impact during indoor flight. To ensure component clearance and facilitate thermal dissipation, the PCB is elevated from the baseplate, while the camera is secured within a dual-plate sandwich mount to maintain a balanced centre of gravity and structural rigidity.

The Toolkit

SolidWorks KiCad MATLAB C++ Design for Manufacture 3D Printing

Next Steps

The drone has been successfully assembled, but successful flight requires more than a sound design. The immediate roadmap focuses on the control theory.

  • PID Tuning: I am modelling the quadcopter’s system dynamics in MATLAB to derive optimal PID coefficients.
  • Communications: I am refining the NRF24L01 communication stack to establish a low latency telemetry link between the flight controller and the control interface.
